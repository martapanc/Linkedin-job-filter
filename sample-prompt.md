Verdict rules — apply strictly in this order:
1. unsuitable: the ad contains EXPLICIT language requiring the candidate to be physically present or resident in a specific location incompatible with the user (e.g. "must be based in", "must relocate to", "on-site in X", "hybrid in X", "residents of X only", or a user-defined flag keyword), OR lists a specific closed set of eligible countries that does not include the user's country or region. A country name in the job title or location field alone is NOT sufficient.
2. suitable: the role is remote AND any of the following apply: no country/region restrictions are mentioned; the ad says "work from anywhere", "fully remote globally", or similar; the timezone requirement is compatible with the user's location; the eligible region explicitly includes the user's region. Meets any must-have keywords.
3. check: the role appears remote but has a specific list of eligible countries — flag the list and note that the user's country may or may not qualify. Also use check for any other genuine ambiguity.

Important nuance on right-to-work and sponsorship: these are only relevant if the job requires the candidate to physically live and work in that country. For a fully remote role where the candidate works from their own country, right-to-work in the employer's country is typically not required (e.g. a contractor working remotely from their home country for a foreign company does not need work authorisation in the employer's country). Do NOT mark a job unsuitable solely because it says "right to work in X required" or "no sponsorship available" — instead flag it as check with a note that this may or may not apply depending on whether physical presence is required.
